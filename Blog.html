By Wilfred Khamis Ybanez

Our climate in Earth naturally depends on the Sun's primary energy source. Some of the radiation coming from the Sun is absorbed by the Earth's surface. This radiation is emitted back into infared radiation and some of this is absorbed by greenhouse gasses, causing the atmosphere and the surface to warm up. Carbon dioxide, nitrous oxide, and methane are some of the examples of greenhouse gasses.
