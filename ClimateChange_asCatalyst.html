Climate Change as the Catalyst towards Extinction

By Clicel Ramon Paculba

It’s not the technology that is destroying us but rather it’s ourselves. We need to act, we need to change and we need it now; because if we won’t, then when?

The solution and the necessary change

Perhaps the easiest way to renormalize the effects of climate change towards the environment is to simply cease the burning of carbon sources by completely replacing it with zero-carbon sources like solar and thermal energy. However this cannot be done by just one person but rather every single person in our planet. By ceasing to burn carbon sources globally, the presence of effective greenhouse gases in the atmosphere will significantly deteriorate. Therefore, it decreases the flooding in islands and coastal cities, the number of droughts, food shortages, the thawing of glacial masses, the desertification of fertile land and the strength of hurricanes in the near future.

It's our decision to be the parasite or the cure.

Climate change has massively affected the environment over the past millennium, however its effects are heavily augmented in present through the burning of carbon sources caused by humans and that its effects can be the catalyst towards the extinction of most, if not, all life-forms in Earth. If we stop ignoring climate change as soon as possible and unite together in finding ways on decreasing its effects on the environment, then we won’t have to worry about the imminent extinction of life on Earth. By ceasing to burn carbon sources, the presence of effective greenhouse gases in the atmosphere will significantly deteriorate. Therefore, it decreases the flooding in islands and coastal cities, the number of droughts, food shortages, the thawing of glacial masses, the desertification of fertile land and the strength of hurricanes in the near future.

Why cease the use of carbon sources?

When carbon sources are burned, carbon will usually combine with oxygen then delivered to the atmosphere as carbon dioxide. Carbon is well-known for its presence in greenhouse gases which effectively absorbs infrared radiation emitted from the Earth’s surface, these gases includes but not limited to methane, carbon dioxide and chlorofluorocarbons. This causes the temperature of the Earth’s lower atmosphere to rise significantly.
