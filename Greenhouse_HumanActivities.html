Greenhouse Gases from Human Activities

By Wilfred Khamis Ybanez

Our climate in Earth naturally depends on the Sun's primary energy source. Some of the radiation coming from the Sun is absorbed by the Earth's surface. This radiation is emitted back into infared radiation and some of this is absorbed by greenhouse gasses, causing the atmosphere and the surface to warm up. Carbon dioxide, nitrous oxide, and methane are some of the examples of greenhouse gasses.

Human Activities Changes Climate

Even though human actions cause most of the greenhouse gasses, a lot of data was gathered and proved that most of the gasses were produced by natural causes. Humans have to influence this natural variations that can affect our precipitation, temperature, and other aspdcts of climate. In order to solve this, scientist have recorded data and observed the patterns to balance the climate with human activities. With our activities that helps produce more greenhouse gasses, we obstruct the process of the balancing of the climate. We can help the researchers progress more if we simply stop activities that produce this gasses. Human activities should be not too much or little but enough to balance the climate.
