We need more action!

By Renz Nathaniel Luyao

We humans have created a very big issue, we have released large amounts of carbon and other greenhouse gases which would result in increase of the temperature of our planet's atmosphere.

The Greenhouse Effect

These gases came not only from the burning of carbon sources and other fossil fuels, but also deforestation and industrial manufacturing. Greenhouse gases are the gases which trap the infrared rays of the sun from entering the atmosphere and it can cause the temperature of the atmosphere to rise which leads to global warming. This is called the greenhouse effect and it is an essential thing to maintain and support life on earth. Many changes can happen when there is a warmer climate because it can bring changes that can affect our agriculture, habitats, power and transportation systems, water supplies, and even our own health and welfare.

How does climate change affect us?

Climate change is a moderate and progressive conversion of our habitat. Today’s population can only contemplate the worst that can happen. However, we can plan ahead so that our future generations will be more prepared and ready for the environmental changes that they will have to face.

The drastic increase in the temperature can cause different changes on Earth. It can lead to strong floods, extreme droughts or intense rain and more severe heat waves. Oceans and glaciers are melting and becoming more acidic while sea levels are rising. All of these changes can affect our environment in future decades and will certainly present challenges to our society.

Conclusion

We need to join and make a change to stop both global warming and activities that can augment climate change. If we will not do something the earth’s temperature will continue to rise and increase until all living creatures on earth would become extinct.

If we all contribute and make a move to control global warming, then our future would be guaranteed. If we as one take a stand and try to end the increase of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, this world would be a safer place to live on for both us and the future generations.
