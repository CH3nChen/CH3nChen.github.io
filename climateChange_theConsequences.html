Climate Change: The Consequences

By Jasmine Villar

Climate Change refers to long-term alteration in global weather patterns, especially the increasing temperature and storm activity, regarded as a potential consequence of the greenhouse effect.

Most of the people have been greatly informed about the how the rate of our changing climate could negatively affect the planet, and humans, the inventions to be more specific hold the greatest cause on this crisis. According to Engineering and Technology Wiki (2015), the relationship between humanity and the environment is a delicately balanced. The Process

If you have observed over these past years, the oceans are getting hotter, expanding and are getting more acidic. The oceans are getting hotter because it is absorbing 90% of the extra heat in the climate. This absorption causes the oceans to expand, resulting to higher sea levels. Weather events are getting more and more extreme, currently Japan is experiencing the heavy rains and strong winds of a super typhoon known as Hagibis that is travelling at a speed of 216 kilometers/hour (134 miles/hour). Heatwaves are getting more frequent worldwide. Summers are getting hotter and winters are getting cooler
